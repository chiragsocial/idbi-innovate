The Problem

63 million MSMEs. A ₹20–25 lakh crore credit gap.

Banks assess credit with traditional documents — ITR, audited balance sheets, bank statements, and a bureau (CIBIL) score.

New-to-Credit (NTC) and New-to-Bank (NTB) MSMEs simply don't have them. No loan history → no bureau score → auto-rejected.

They aren't uncreditworthy. They're invisible.

Who gets left out

Kirana & retailFabrication units TransportersTraders Gig workersAgri

Rich businesses, thin files. The bank has no lens to see them.

A business with no loan documents still leaves a rich digital footprint.

GST filings · UPI flows · bank statements (AA) · EPF payroll · electricity · fuel · FASTag · Udyam. Read them right, and creditworthiness is visible in seconds.

The Solution

An explainable Financial Health Card for the underwriter

Aggregate

Score

Six financial-health pillars → a single adaptive score → GREEN AMBER RED.

Decision-support, not auto-approve — exactly what the AMA mandated: “the AI gives the reason and logic; the credit decision lies with the underwriter.”

Why it matters

Templated from explicit values — reproducible & auditable for RBI / fair-lending.

SHAP on the model maps to the same six pillars — rule-based and model-based explanations corroborate.

Optional Gemini narration only rephrases — never touches the decision.

Data can be wrong. We check it.

Independent revenue estimates from GST, UPI and bank must agree. Divergence → confidence drops → routed to manual review.

Flagged: “UPI implies ₹12 L/mo but bank implies ₹3.5 L/mo (44% variation) — verify before relying on the score.”

Directly answers the AMA concern: “wrong account number → false data.”
